What is a host home?

Care happens where it's most natural — at home.

A Host Home is a Medicaid-funded living arrangement under Wyoming's DD Waiver where an adult with a developmental disability lives in the private home of an approved provider — often a parent, sibling, grandparent, or close friend.

Instead of moving to a group home or institution, the individual moves in with a host family — so they can live in a more natural family setting where daily life feels like, well, life. The provider receives a daily payment for offering room, board, supervision, and habilitative support based on the individual's authorized service level.

In Wyoming, Host Home is delivered under the Community Living Service (CLS) — at the Basic, Level 3, or Level 4 daily rate, depending on the support level the participant is authorized for.

The short version

If a loved one with a DD diagnosis lives in your home and you provide their daily care, the State of Wyoming will pay you for it. Simpl Care is the agency that makes it possible — and makes it easy.

FAQ

Common questions from new Host Home providers.

Can I be paid to care for my own family member?

Yes — and most of the providers we work with are. Wyoming's DD Waiver allows family members, friends, and other close relationships to serve as paid Host Home providers, with one important exception: parents, step-parents, and legal guardians of the participant cannot serve as the Host Home provider.

Siblings, grandparents, aunts and uncles, cousins, and family friends are all generally eligible. We'll confirm eligibility for your specific situation on a quick consultation call.

What's the difference between Host Home and other DD Waiver services?

Host Home is a residential service paid as a daily rate that includes around-the-clock supervision, room, board, and habilitative support — the participant lives full-time in your home.

Other services like Personal Care or Companion Services are billed by the hour and don't require the participant to live with you. Many of our providers combine services depending on what their family needs.

How long does it take to get approved?

From the time you complete your trainings and pass your background check, most providers are billable within a few weeks. The biggest variable is how quickly the certifications and home walkthrough get completed on your end — we move as fast as you do.

I'm with another agency. How does switching work?

It's the smoothest part of what we do. We coordinate the agency change with the participant's case manager, transfer service authorizations, and onboard you to our system in parallel — so there's no gap in pay or support. Most switches are complete within 30 days.

Do I need a license or specific training?

No professional license is required. You'll need CPR & First Aid certification (any state-approved provider) and you'll complete Simpl Care's online onboarding trainings, which satisfy the DD Waiver requirements. We provide everything you need and walk you through it.
